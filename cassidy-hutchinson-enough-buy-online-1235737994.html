“Enough” follows Hutchinson’s early life as the daughter of a working-class family in Pennington, New Jersey to her rapid rise in the country’s foremost political ranks. The book particularly follows her journey in landing a vital position as assistant to then-Chief of Staff Mark Meadows at the center of the Trump White House in Washington D.C. at the age of just 24.

The memoir details the events of January 6, 2021, as well as its aftermath. The 384-page book also highlights Hutchinson’s role as a key witness during the House Select Committee’s hearings on the attack on the U.S. Capitol.

In a recent interview with “The View,” Hutchinson discussed her new book, adding that her past admiration of the former-president changed following the events of January 6 and her split from the White House, adding that she had to break her “unspoken cloak of loyalty” to Donald Trump.

“My life has definitely changed in a variety of ways. All good ways, but there has been a learning curve with all of this,” Hutchinson says on “The View.” “In this period, I had separated from Trump World, very publicly on June 28. But after that, it was this period of, sort of, trying to figure out how I was going to tell my story and tell my story effectively and responsibly.”
