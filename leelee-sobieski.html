Leelee Sobieski (Actress) - On This Day

Full Name: Liliane Rudabet Gloria Elsveta Sobieski
Profession: Actress

Biography: Leelee Sobieski is an American artist and retired actress. She was discovered by a talent scout at a young age and landed her first roles in "Interview with the Vampire" (1994) and TV movies like "Reunion" (1994) and "A Horse for Danny" (1995).

She was born on June 10, 1983, in New York City to a French-born painter father and an American film producer mother. Sobieski's breakthrough role came in "Deep Impact" (1998) at age 15, which brought her to the attention of many casting directors.

She achieved critical acclaim and award nominations for her performances in films like "Eyes Wide Shut" (1999), "Never Been Kissed" (1999), and the TV movies "Joan of Arc" (1999), for which she received Emmy and Golden Globe nominations, and "Uprising" (2001), which earned her a Golden Globe nomination.

Other notable films include "Here on Earth" (2000), "Joy Ride" (2001), and "The Glass House" (2001). Sobieski continued to work in films and television until her retirement in 2012. In 2009, she married fashion designer Adam John Kimmel, and they have two children together.

After retiring from acting, Sobieski transitioned to an art career, debuting her solo exhibit "Channels" in 2018 and working in various mediums like painting, sculpture, and virtual reality.
