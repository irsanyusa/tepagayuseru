Zhavia from The Four recently cancelled the New York City stop of her tour! She was set to perform at Baby’s All Right in Brooklyn on Thursday, April 4th. And the reason why is less than clear.

Here is her announcement from Instagram:

Zhavia kicked off her “100 Ways” tour last month, with a sold out show at The Troubadour in LA. The show also celebrated her 18th birthday! Fans across the country were excited to finally see the up-and-coming star live.

So this cancellation was obviously a bummer for her fans on the East Coast. Zhavia says she cancelled the concert this Thursday due to a “REALLY huge opportunity” that she “couldn’t turn down.” But cancelling a sold out show in New York City is a big deal! Especially for a star who is just making a name for herself in the industry. It must be something truly HUGE to justify cancelling the show.

Zhavia has really been on the rise since her appearance on Season 1 of The Four though. First, she signed a record deal with Sony’s Columbia records. Then, she was featured in “Welcome to the Party,” a track on the Deadpool 2 soundtrack, with Diplo, French Montana, and Lil Pump. Zhavia has since released a few other singles to her fans’ overwhelming acclaim!

East Coast fans can keep their eyes peeled for another announcement from Zhavia though. She thanked her fans and teased that she will play a show at the venue “very soon.”
