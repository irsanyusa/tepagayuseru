Richard Gere and Mohammed Al Turki to Be Honored at amfARs Venice Film Festival Gala (EXCLU

Kelly Rowland and Rumer Willis will perform during the benefit

AmfAR, The Foundation for AIDS Research, has announced this year’s honorees at its upcoming Venice International Film Festival gala, amfAR Venezia.

Richard Gere will be recognized with the Award of Inspiration. “Few in the entertainment industry have done as much as Richard Gere to advance the fight against AIDS,” amfAR CEO Kevin Robert Frost said in a statement on Monday morning. “His humanitarian instincts and deep desire to help those on the margins of society, make him an inspiration to us all here at amfAR.” 

Film producer, businessman, philanthropist and former Red Sea Film Foundation CEO Mohammed Al Turki will be presented with the Philanthropic Leadership Award. “Thanks in part to Mohammed Al Turki’s leadership, The Red Sea International Film Festival has been a loyal supporter of amfAR’s work for many years, and through their generosity and partnership, they have helped advance our lifesaving research,” Frost said.

The HIV/AIDS organization also revealed Monday that Rumer Willis will perform during the evening. She joins previously announced performer Kelly Rowland.

AmfAR Venezia will take place at Hangar Nicelli, Lido di Venezia, on Sept. 1. Presenting sponsors include World Gold Council and The Red Sea International Film Festival, with Chopard and Campari as signature sponsors. San Clemente Palace Kempinski is the official hotel partner. The event is also supported by Boroli Wines and RUMOR Rosé. 

AmfAR is one of the world’s leading nonprofit organizations dedicated to the support of AIDS research, HIV prevention, treatment education and advocacy. amfAR has invested more than $635 million in its programs and has awarded more than 3,800 grants to research teams worldwide since it was launched in 1985.

The 81st edition of the Venice Film Festival runs Aug. 28 to Sept. 7.
