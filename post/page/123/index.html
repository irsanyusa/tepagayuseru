How BBL surgery could lead to a stroke

published on July 31, 2024
Fat embolism occurs when fat particles enter the bloodstream, potentially traveling to the brain or lungs. In the brain, this can mimic stroke symptoms, presenting a serious health threat. Fat embolism, although rare, can manifest through symptoms akin to a stroke, including confusion, rapid breathing, and an abrupt onset of neurological deficits. These symptoms underscore the critical nature of the surgery's risk. The procedure's safety largely hinges on the technique used; injecting fat into or near large blood vessels increases the risk of fat embolism, highlighting the importance of choosing a highly skilled surgeon.
